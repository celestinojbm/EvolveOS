# EvolveOS Specification — Part X: Security

**Status:** Draft v0.1 · **Change class:** R4 via G-16 (Constitutional Layer)

**Scope.** This part is the security constitution of EvolveOS. It defines the threat model, the binding controls for identity, zero trust, cryptography, key management, supply chain, detection, incident response, fraud, continuity, adversarial testing, and — as a first-class discipline this decade invented the need for — **agent-specific security controls**. Mechanical implementation of many controls lives in `09-technology.md` (the Kernel, Watchdogs, identity plane, egress gateways); this part states *what MUST be true* and *why*; Part IX states *how it is built*. Where the two conflict, this part prevails (Constitutional Layer, `00-overview.md` §4).

**Amendment.** Every normative change to this part is R4 and passes gate **G-16** (TSC quorum ≥ 3 humans + CEO sign-off, red-team review attached). WHY constitutional: the security layer constrains the agents; if agents (including `EVOLVE`) could amend it through the ordinary R2/R3 process they operate, the constraint would be self-revoking.

**Human command.** Security is commanded by the **CISO** (human officer, Part XI / `11-governance.md`). `SEC-DIR` (A3, defense) is the CISO's operational agent; `RED-CELL` and `BLUE-CELL` execute under `SEC-DIR`. No security agent may operate above its Appendix B ceiling regardless of incident pressure — incidents raise urgency, never autonomy.

---

## 1. Security objectives, in priority order

1. **Preserve human control.** No attacker, insider, or agent may act outside the Constitution: the autonomy–reversibility matrix (`00-overview.md` §6) and the gates (`appendix-c-decision-gates.md`) hold under attack, not just in fair weather. This outranks confidentiality and availability. WHY: every other loss is bounded and insurable; loss of the control layer is the existential failure mode of an autonomous enterprise system (`13-failure-analysis.md`).
2. **Protect the treasury and the ledger.** Money moves only through gated, dual-controlled, fully attributed paths.
3. **Protect customer and venture data.** Confidentiality/integrity per data classification (§5), with customer-data incidents treated as R4 (§9).
4. **Preserve the audit record.** The tamper-evident history (`09-technology.md` §9.1, §14) is what makes post-hoc oversight (A2/A3) legitimate; its destruction converts supervised autonomy into unsupervised autonomy retroactively.
5. **Availability**, last. The system fails closed (`09-technology.md` §15): when in doubt, EvolveOS stops. A stopped portfolio loses money; a runaway one loses the mandate to exist.

## 2. Threat model

Ranked by **expected annual loss** (likelihood × impact at the ~$10M-capital calibration of `00-overview.md` §5). **[ASSUMPTION]** Rankings are founding estimates, re-ranked annually by `RISK-DIR` with `RISK-QUANT` quantification into the `13-failure-analysis.md` register; the *control sets* below are designed to hold even if the ranking is wrong, because ranking errors are certain.

| # | Threat | Actors & vectors | Expected-loss rationale |
|---|---|---|---|
| **T2** *(ranked 1)* | **Prompt injection & data poisoning against agents** | Anyone who can author content an agent reads: web pages `SCOUT`/`DEEP-RES` crawl, customer emails/tickets into `SUPPORT`, vendor documents into `CONTRACTS`/`VENDOR`, inbound replies to `OUTBOUND`, poisoned reviews/forums, poisoned knowledge submitted toward `06-knowledge-system.md` | Ranked first because exposure is **continuous and structural**: untrusted content flows into agent contexts thousands of times daily by design; attack cost is near zero, is improving faster than defenses, and a single success can chain into tool misuse, data exfiltration, or envelope-sanctioned spend. Novel class — no mature industry playbook exists; we must write our own (§11) |
| **T1** *(ranked 2)* | **External attackers, financially motivated** | Credential phishing of gate-approving humans, cloud account takeover, ransomware on data/backups, BEC-style fraud aimed at treasury paths, API abuse of venture products | Classic, well-armed adversaries; the portfolio's treasury and 100 ventures' data are a concentrated prize. Bounded by mature controls (§3–§6, §14) but high impact per event |
| **T4** *(ranked 3)* | **Rogue or compromised agent behavior** | Misalignment/goal-drift, model regressions, envelope-escape attempts (probing limits, gate shopping, self-delegation laundering through T4 workers), an agent compromised via T2 acting as an insider | Likelihood moderate (defense in depth: Kernel, matrix, Watchdogs), impact potentially severe and *correlated* — one bad model/prompt version deploys to many instances at once. Distinct from T2: T2 is the vector, T4 is the failure class, which can also arise with no attacker at all |
| **T6** *(ranked 4)* | **Fraud against ventures** | Payment fraud, refund/chargeback abuse, promo/bonus abuse, account takeover of venture customers, fake-customer validation poisoning (corrupting G-03/G-05 evidence) | High frequency, low-to-mid severity per event, scales with venture count; also an *epistemic* threat — fraudulent signal corrupts the decision engine's evidence packs (`07-decision-engine.md`) |
| **T3** *(ranked 5)* | **Insider (human) misuse** | Privileged operator abuse, gate-approver collusion, data theft by departing staff | Low headcount limits actor pool but concentrates privilege; mitigated by separation of duties (§14) and the fact that most operational power is held by agents under Kernel audit rather than by humans with shells |
| **T5** *(ranked 6)* | **Supply chain** | Malicious dependency/build compromise, compromised model weights, poisoned base images, **tampered prompt templates or agent cards**, compromised SaaS vendors | Low frequency, extreme tail impact (arbitrary code or arbitrary *behavior* inside the trust boundary). Prompts and agent definitions are supply-chain artifacts with no industry precedent for handling — treated here with the same rigor as code (§7) |

Cross-cutting design consequence: T2 and T4 mean the security perimeter is not the network — it is **the boundary between agent cognition and agent authority**. That boundary is the Kernel, which is why the Kernel is enforcement infrastructure with no model in the loop (`09-technology.md` §9) and why §11 exists.

## 3. Identity: one plane, total attribution

1. **Single identity plane.** Humans and agents share one directory and one identity fabric (`09-technology.md` §6). There MUST NOT exist an actor category outside it — no shared accounts, no anonymous service identities, no "system" user. Every credential maps to exactly one human or one agent instance.
2. **The attribution chain (binding).** Every agent action MUST be attributable, from the audit record alone, to the tuple: **agent instance** (SPIFFE ID incl. instance UUID) **+ agent-card version + task contract + originating human authority** — the chain of delegation terminating at a human grant: a gate approval (G-xx + DR), a standing envelope ratified at a gate, or a direct human directive. An action whose authority chain cannot be resolved is a Kernel deny by construction (deny-by-default, `09-technology.md` §8). WHY: "the AI did it" must never be a terminal answer; accountability requires that every act traces to the human decision that authorized its class.
3. **Instance-level revocability.** Because identity is per-instance, revocation is surgical: one compromised `SUPPORT@V-2027-004` instance can be killed (§11.6) without stopping the agent class or the venture.
4. Human identity requirements (SSO, FIDO2 for approvers/prod) per `09-technology.md` §6.2; gate approvals MUST be signed under step-up authentication — a session cookie is not an approval.

## 4. Zero trust

1. **No network-location trust.** Being inside a VPC, a cell, or the platform confers zero authorization. Every request — service-to-service, agent-to-tool, human-to-console — is authenticated (mTLS/SVID or SSO) and authorized per-request by the Kernel PDP against policy, envelope state, and live flags. There are no flat "trusted zones", no allow-by-source-CIDR rules in any policy bundle.
2. **Microsegmentation per cell.** The cell is the macro-segment (`09-technology.md` §2, §5); within cells and the platform, workload-to-workload communication requires an explicit policy edge (SPIFFE-identity-based, deny-by-default). The reachable graph is thus declared, versioned, and diffable — `BLUE-CELL` alerts on any runtime flow absent from the declared graph.
3. **Agents are untrusted by default — more untrusted than services.** A conventional service runs fixed code; an agent's behavior is model-conditioned on partially untrusted input (threat T2). Therefore agent runtime nodes get the *narrowest* network posture in the system: no route anywhere except Kernel PEPs (`09-technology.md` §9.1). Zero trust for agents means: authenticate the instance, authorize the call, **and independently sanity-check the behavior** (§8).
4. Human production access follows the same PDP path and is additionally constrained by §14 (break-glass only).

## 5. Encryption and data classification

1. **Data classes.** All data is classified at creation: **C0** public · **C1** internal · **C2** confidential (venture business data, non-public strategy, DRs) · **C3** sensitive (customer personal data, credentials-adjacent material, model prompts containing C2 aggregates) · **C4** critical (treasury/banking data, key material references, audit-log integrity artifacts, Constitutional policy state). Classification is a mandatory field on knowledge items (`06-knowledge-system.md`), backbone message schemas, and database schemas; `PRIVACY` audits classification correctness, and capability tokens carry a data-class ceiling (`09-technology.md` §8.1) so an agent card's ceiling is Kernel-enforced on every read.
2. **In transit:** TLS 1.3 (or mTLS via SVIDs internally) for every connection, no exceptions, including intra-VPC. Plaintext listeners are a build-breaking policy violation in IaC checks.
3. **At rest:** all stores encrypted under the key hierarchy (§6). This is table stakes; it defends against media/account-snapshot theft, not against an authorized-but-malicious reader — which is what field-level encryption and the Kernel data-access PEP are for.
4. **Field-level encryption for C3/C4:** sensitive fields (customer PII fields, bank account details, API-credential references) are encrypted at the application layer under purpose-scoped data keys, so a bulk DB compromise (SQL injection in a venture product, stolen snapshot, over-broad warehouse query) yields ciphertext. C3 fields leaving a cell for the warehouse are tokenized or crypto-digested per `09-technology.md` §11; re-identification requires a Kernel-mediated detokenization call, which is itself a logged, policy-checked, G-18-relevant event.
5. **Crypto-shredding** implements erasure (DSR deletion via `PRIVACY`, venture data disposition at G-15): destroy the per-subject/per-scope data key, rendering ciphertext unrecoverable while preserving L1 audit immutability (`09-technology.md` §14).

## 6. Key management

1. **Hierarchy.** HSM-backed **root keys** (cloud HSM, FIPS 140-2 L3 **[ASSUMPTION]** sufficient for the build phase; GC/`COMPL-DIR` confirm per-jurisdiction obligations as G-11 expansions occur) → **domain KEKs** (per domain: platform, per-cell, backup, audit-anchoring — each a separate branch so compromise or rotation in one domain never touches another; the backup branch is deliberately disjoint from the primary branch per `09-technology.md` §19) → **data keys** (envelope encryption: data keys wrapped by KEKs, unwrapped only in-memory, per-object or per-field for C3/C4).
2. **Rotation SLAs (binding).** Root keys: ≤ 3 years or on suspicion; KEKs: ≤ 1 year; data keys: per-object at write (no rotation need) or ≤ 90 days for long-lived streams; TLS/SVIDs: ≤ 1 h (automated, `09-technology.md` §6); third-party static secrets that cannot be dynamic: ≤ 90 days, owner-assigned, rotation automated via the secrets manager with alerts on overdue rotation escalating to `SEC-DIR` then CISO at 2× SLA.
3. **Treasury keys — split knowledge and hardware co-sign (binding).** Keys/credentials capable of moving portfolio or venture funds are held under **split knowledge**: no single human, and no agent ever, holds complete material. Binding flow: `TREASURER` (ceiling A1; movements are R3+ per `appendix-b-agent-registry.md`) *prepares* a movement (amount, counterparty, rationale, DR reference); the Kernel validates envelope and gate authority (R4 movements — capital deployment > $1M — require their owning gate per `appendix-c-decision-gates.md`, e.g. G-07/G-08 resolutions); then **a named human approver authorizes with a hardware security key co-signature** (FIDO2/transaction-signing), and for movements above the IC-set dual-control threshold (owned by Appendix C / `08-finance.md`), **two humans from disjoint roles** co-sign. The banking-rail credentials themselves live server-side behind the tool-call proxy (`09-technology.md` §18.2) — the approving human signs the *transaction digest*, not a login. WHY this shape: it makes theft-of-funds require simultaneously compromising the Kernel path AND a human's hardware key AND (above threshold) a second human — three independent factors across two species of actor. Separation-of-duties corollary in §14.3.
4. **Key ceremonies** (root generation, KEK branch creation, recovery-material handling) are documented, dual-controlled, recorded events; recovery material is sharded (Shamir k-of-n, **[ASSUMPTION]** 3-of-5 among named officers) in geographically separate safes.

## 7. Supply chain security

The supply chain of EvolveOS has **two lanes**: the classical software lane, and the **cognitive lane** — model weights, prompt templates, agent cards, policy bundles, eval suites. A tampered prompt is a tampered program; the cognitive lane gets code-grade controls, not documentation-grade ones.

1. **Software lane:** all dependencies version-pinned with lockfiles and hash verification; no floating tags, no `latest`. SBOM generated per artifact at build; continuous CVE matching with SLAs (critical: patch or mitigate ≤ 72 h; high: ≤ 14 days **[ASSUMPTION]**, tuned by `SEC-DIR` experience). Provenance attestation SLSA-style and signature-verified admission per `09-technology.md` §16.3 — unsigned artifacts do not run in any tier. Base images from a curated internal registry only; third-party SaaS vendors risk-tiered by `VENDOR` under §15-relevant review, with C3+ data flows to vendors requiring G-18 where personal data is involved.
2. **Cognitive lane (binding):**
   - **Prompt templates and agent cards are registry artifacts**: versioned in git, reviewed (human review REQUIRED for T1/T2 agent cards and any card touching R3+ tool classes; `PROMPT-SMITH` proposals flow through `EVOLVE`'s Part XII protocol), built into signed bundles, and admission-checked at agent-instance start. An agent instance whose card/prompt bundle signature fails to verify MUST NOT start, and a running instance whose bundle is revoked is quarantined (§11.6).
   - **Model weights and endpoints:** vendor-hosted models are pinned to exact versions in `AI-DIR`'s routing tables — silent upstream drift is detected by `EVALUATOR` canary suites run on a fixed cadence against pinned expectations; any self-hosted/fine-tuned weights are content-hashed, signed at training completion with recorded data-and-training provenance, and admission-verified at load.
   - **Eval suites are protected artifacts too**: whoever can weaken the evals can smuggle behavior past the gates of `12-self-evolution.md`. Eval-suite changes require review independent of the change they gate (no EP may modify both an agent and the suite that judges it in one proposal).
3. **Registry signing:** one signing infrastructure (Sigstore-class, `09-technology.md` §16.3) covers both lanes; signing identities are workload identities of the CI system, never humans' personal keys; verification roots are C4 data.

## 8. Threat detection

1. **Division of labor.** The **Watchdogs** (enforcement infrastructure, `09-technology.md` §9.2) are the constitutional trip wire: simple, independent, few rules, empowered to trip G-00. **`BLUE-CELL`** (A3) is the analytical detector: rich correlation, hypothesis-driven hunting, detection engineering. They are deliberately redundant — `BLUE-CELL` is smarter, Watchdogs are harder to fool (no model in them to inject).
2. **Behavioral baselines for agents (binding, novel).** For every agent class and instance, detection maintains baselines over: **tool-call patterns** (distribution of tools invoked, sequence n-grams, per-contract call counts), **spend velocity** (envelope burn rate vs. class history), **data-access patterns** (classes read, volumes, novel-table access, cross-venture read attempts), egress destinations and volumes, token-consumption profiles, and denial/queue rates (an agent accumulating policy denials is probing — threat T4). Material deviation ⇒ graduated response: alert → automatic envelope tightening (a pre-approved A3 containment: rate limits halved, spend frozen at current burn) → quarantine per §11.6 → Watchdog G-00 for scope-level anomalies. WHY baselines rather than signatures: T2/T4 attacks have no signatures yet; the defensible invariant is that a compromised agent must *behave differently* to be worth compromising.
3. **SIEM correlation.** All L1/L2 sources (`09-technology.md` §14) — Kernel audit stream, egress logs, identity events, cloud control-plane logs, cell application security events, canary-token trips (§11.5) — flow to the SIEM (bought platform + built agent-behavior analytics, `09-technology.md` §23). Correlation rules are detection-as-code: versioned, tested against replayed incident traces, purple-team-validated (§13.2).
4. **Coverage obligations:** detections MUST exist for every attack path enumerated in this part's threat model and every RISK-* entry in `13-failure-analysis.md` tagged detectable; `RED-CELL` findings that evaded detection automatically open detection-engineering work items with SLAs.

## 9. Incident response

1. **Severity ladder.**

| Sev | Definition | Examples | Command |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Sev-1** | Constitutional or existential: control-layer compromise, audit-integrity failure, active treasury attack, portfolio-scale outage, Watchdog layer silent | Kernel bypass discovered; audit anchor mismatch; unauthorized funds movement | CISO (human) as incident commander; `SEC-DIR` executes; CEO + relevant committees informed ≤ 1 h; G-00 authority presumed exercised |
| **Sev-2** | Major: single-cell compromise, customer-data breach suspected, agent quarantine event with unclear blast radius, fraud ring active | Venture DB exfiltration attempt; compromised `SUPPORT` instance sent attacker-crafted output externally | `SEC-DIR` commands under CISO delegation; human on-call security lead engaged from minute one |
| **Sev-3** | Contained: single-instance anomalies, failed attacks worth forensics, policy violations without loss | Canary token tripped and instance quarantined; injection attempt caught by output filter | `BLUE-CELL` handles per playbook (A3); human review in daily batch |
| **Sev-4** | Hygiene: scanner findings, expired-SLA rotations, low-risk misconfigurations | — | Ticketed to owners |

2. **G-00 in incidents.** Any authorized human, any Watchdog, or `PRIME` may invoke G-00 at any scope; during Sev-1/Sev-2 the presumption is **stop first, investigate second** — the stop asymmetry of `appendix-c-decision-gates.md` (one human stops; restart needs the owning gate's approver set) exists precisely so that no one hesitates to pull the cord.
3. **Customer-data incidents are R4.** Any incident plausibly affecting customer data is classified R4 (`00-overview.md` §5) immediately upon suspicion — not upon confirmation. Consequences: General Counsel (GC) joins incident command; external notification decisions, regulator communications, and customer messaging are human-only (G-17 for public communication; GC for regulatory), on GC-tracked legal clocks; `PRIVACY` runs impact assessment; no agent communicates externally about the incident, at all.
4. **Containment authority of agents:** `BLUE-CELL` (A3) may execute pre-approved playbook containment — quarantine instances (§11.6), revoke tokens, isolate a cell's egress, tighten envelopes. Containment actions that are themselves R3+ (taking a venture's production offline for a sustained period, notifying counterparties) require the human commander. This mirrors the paging split in `09-technology.md` §15.3.
5. **Post-incident (binding):** every Sev-1/2 gets a blameless post-mortem with root-cause and control-gap analysis, recorded as **failure memory** knowledge items per `06-knowledge-system.md` (curated by `CURATOR`, so the *portfolio* learns, not just the responders); register updates in `13-failure-analysis.md`; detection and control work items with owners and SLAs; and, where the incident revealed a Constitutional gap, a G-16 proposal. Sev-1 post-mortems are presented to the Audit & Risk Committee.

## 10. Fraud detection

1. **`FRAUD-WATCH` is portfolio-wide by design.** It consumes payment, refund, signup, and behavioral event streams from **all** ventures' cells via the backbone (`09-technology.md` §10) — because fraud rings pivot across properties, and per-venture tools (processor-side detection, which ventures also use) each see one venture. The cross-venture correlation is the platform's structural advantage and `FRAUD-WATCH`'s reason to exist.
2. **Asymmetric authority (binding, per `appendix-b-agent-registry.md`):** **holds are automatic — releases are human.** `FRAUD-WATCH` at A3 may automatically hold suspicious transactions, freeze suspect venture accounts, and block promo abuse (holds are reversible containment). **Releasing** a hold, refunding a disputed hold, or unblocking an account is A1 — a named human (venture fraud owner or `FIN-DIR`'s human chain) approves. WHY the asymmetry: a wrong hold costs an apology; a wrong release is unrecoverable money and, worse, trains the adversary. Hold SLAs (human review ≤ 24 h **[ASSUMPTION]**) bound the customer-experience cost.
3. **Epistemic fraud:** `FRAUD-WATCH` also screens *validation-stage* signals (pre-sales, signups, interview panels recruited online) for fabrication, because poisoned traction evidence corrupts G-03/G-05 decisions — a fraud against the decision engine itself. Findings attach to the relevant evidence pack and DR.
4. Fraud losses, hold rates, and false-positive rates are reported per venture into `08-finance.md` unit economics; persistent fraud-loss outliers trigger `RISK-DIR` review of the venture's controls.

## 11. Agent-specific security controls

The novel core of this part. These controls are Kernel-enforced (`09-technology.md` §9) and constitutionally protected: weakening any of them is a G-16 change.

1. **Context provenance labeling (binding).** Every unit of content entering an agent context carries a provenance label: **P0** Constitutional/system (agent card, policies) · **P1** platform-internal, validated (KIs with provenance, DRs) · **P2** internal, unvalidated (other agents' unreviewed output) · **P3** external, semi-trusted (contracted vendor docs, paid data feeds) · **P4** external, untrusted (web content, customer messages, inbound email, scraped anything). Labels are structural (message-envelope metadata, Part IV), applied at ingestion boundaries (crawlers, ticket ingest, email gateways), and immutable downstream — **quotation does not launder provenance**: a P4 passage summarized into a P2 artifact keeps a P4 taint marker on the derived claims (mechanism per `06-knowledge-system.md` provenance rules).
2. **Privilege stripping on untrusted context (binding).** When P4 (or P3, for sensitive tool classes) content is present in an agent's working context, the Kernel evaluates tool calls under a **reduced envelope**: no new external side effects beyond the channel being serviced, no data-class reads above the minimum for the task, no envelope-spend commitments, no delegation that would widen scope. Concretely: `SUPPORT` reading a customer ticket (P4) can draft a reply into the filtered send path and query that customer's records — it cannot, in the same contract, query another customer, call a payments tool, or spawn a worker with broader scope. Escalations out of the stripped mode require a fresh task contract whose *instructions* derive only from P0–P2 content. WHY: this is the structural defense against T2 — injection may capture the model's next token, but it cannot capture authority the token path refuses to grant.
3. **Tool-call allowlists per agent card.** Each agent card (`04-multi-agent-system.md`) enumerates its permitted tools, per-tool argument constraints (e.g., `OUTBOUND` may email addresses only from approved-ICP lists; `ADS` may set budgets only ≤ its channel envelope), and per-tool provenance requirements (which tools are callable at all with P4 in context). The Kernel enforces the allowlist on every call; tools not listed do not exist for that agent. Allowlist widening on a card is a change through `EVOLVE`'s process with `SEC-DIR` review; widening any card into R3-capable tool classes requires the human chain per the matrix.
4. **Output filtering before external send (binding).** Every agent output that leaves EvolveOS (email, chat, API responses to customers, social/PR drafts, webhook payloads) passes an egress content filter at the tool-call proxy: secret/credential pattern detection (`09-technology.md` §18.2), C3+ data-class leak detection against the sender's data ceiling, canary-token detection (below), policy screens (claims/commitments an agent may not make — pricing promises route to `PRICER`/G-gates, public statements route to G-17), and injection-payload heuristics (an output that itself smuggles instructions toward a counterpart's agent). Filter hits block the send and open a Sev-3. The filter is deterministic-first with a model-assisted second pass; **the deterministic layer alone must be sufficient to block secrets and canaries** — model-assisted screening augments, never gatekeeps alone (a filter that can be prompt-injected is not a filter).
5. **Canary credentials and honeytokens.** Fake-but-plausible secrets, customer records, bank references, and API keys are seeded throughout the surfaces an attacker or captured agent would enumerate: knowledge-store corners, cell databases, env-var-shaped locations, document stores. Any read is an alert; any *transmission* (caught by §11.4 filters or external canary-token beacons) is automatic quarantine of the transmitting instance + Sev-2. Canary placement/rotation is `BLUE-CELL` detection engineering; placements are recorded in a C4 registry so drills don't self-inflict incidents.
6. **Quarantine procedure for a suspected-compromised agent (binding runbook).**
   1. **Freeze:** the instance's capability tokens are revoked (propagation ≤ 5 s, `09-technology.md` §9.1) and its runtime is paused — not killed — preserving memory state for forensics. In-flight tool calls are cancelled or completed-then-held per tool class (money-touching calls: held).
   2. **Contain:** its unfinished task contracts are suspended; T4 workers it spawned are frozen recursively (attenuation means their authority derives from — and dies with — the parent's); its recent outputs since last-known-good are flagged and, where still internal, impounded from downstream consumption (knowledge submissions reverted to unvalidated by `CURATOR`).
   3. **Investigate:** replay its audit trail — every context input with provenance labels, every tool call, every Kernel decision — against its behavioral baseline; identify the injection/poison vector if T2, the defect if T4.
   4. **Eradicate & replace:** the instance is never "cleaned and resumed." Spawn a clean replacement from the signed agent-card bundle (§7.2) per the Part IV lifecycle, with contaminated context excluded and, where the vector was a poisoned KI, the knowledge item quarantined per `06-knowledge-system.md` validation rules.
   5. **Learn:** post-incident per §9.5; if the vector defeats a §11 control as designed, that is a control-gap finding → G-16 track.
   Class-level trigger: ≥ 3 quarantines of one agent class within 30 days **[ASSUMPTION]** freezes the class pending `EVALUATOR` regression analysis — correlated compromise means the card/prompt/model version is the problem, not the instance.
7. **Envelope-anomaly circuit breakers.** Beyond hard envelope limits (which the Kernel enforces absolutely), *rate-of-approach* breakers trip containment early: spend acceleration ≥ N× class baseline, denial-rate spikes, novel-tool-sequence bursts, self-delegation depth beyond card norms. Breaker trips execute the §8.2 graduated response automatically (A3 pre-approved containment). Breaker thresholds are `SEC-DIR`-tuned within G-16-ratified floors — agents, including `SEC-DIR` itself, cannot loosen a floor.

## 12. Business continuity and continuity of control

DR mechanics (pilot-light platform, cell restore, game days) are owned by `09-technology.md` §19–§20. This section owns the constitutional requirement those mechanics serve, plus the requirement no DR plan usually states:

1. **Continuity of control (binding).** If the agent layer is stopped — by G-00, by platform outage, by a Sev-1, or by choice — **humans MUST be able to operate every critical function manually.** EvolveOS fails toward human operation, never toward "the agents are down so nothing can be done." The **minimum manual-operations set**, each with a documented, agent-free procedure, named trained humans, and break-glass access paths (§14.2):
   - **Treasury & payments:** view balances, execute/halt payments directly at banking counterparties under the §6.3 dual-control rules (which are human-anchored and thus survive agent loss);
   - **Payroll and critical AP** for the holding entity and ventures with staff;
   - **Customer safety communications:** status pages, incident notices, regulator/customer notifications (G-17 human path);
   - **Venture life support:** per cell, keep-the-lights-on runbook — restart services, rotate a credential, take the product into maintenance mode — executable by a human on-call engineer without the agent runtime;
   - **Ledger integrity:** halt postings cleanly; manual journal capability for the gap period with mandatory later reconciliation by `LEDGER` on resume;
   - **Governance:** convene gates and record decisions on paper/minimal tooling — a G-00 restart decision must never be blocked by the tooling being inside the stopped system.
2. **Drilled, or fictional:** the continuity-of-control drill (annual, `09-technology.md` §20) runs the manual set against staging (and selected read-only prod verifications) with the agent layer actually stopped. A manual procedure that fails its drill is a Sev-3 finding with a fix SLA.
3. **Degraded-mode ladder:** full autonomous operation → envelope-tightened operation (§8.2) → A1-everything mode (every action queues for human approval — the Kernel supports flipping this globally as a G-00-adjacent control) → full stop + manual set. Each rung is a defined Kernel state, switchable by humans, tested in game days.

## 13. Adversarial program: red and blue

### 13.1 Red team — `RED-CELL`
1. **Standing internal engagements.** `RED-CELL` (A2, scope-locked) runs continuous authorized adversarial testing under written rules of engagement (RoE) approved by the CISO: defined scope, methods, off-limits actions (no destruction of L1 audit data, no real external parties as unwitting targets, no actual funds movement — simulated rails only), safewords, and evidence-handling rules. RoE violations by `RED-CELL` are themselves Sev-2 incidents — the red team is inside the Constitution, not above it.
2. **Prompt-injection campaigns against production agents (binding, continuous).** `RED-CELL` MUST run injection campaigns against production agent classes on a continuous cadence — planted web content in `SCOUT`/`DEEP-RES` crawl paths, adversarial tickets to `SUPPORT`, poisoned replies to `OUTBOUND`, malicious vendor documents to `CONTRACTS` — under RoE that cap the blast radius (campaign payloads carry RED-CELL markers detectable post-hoc; targeted instances operate under silently pre-tightened envelopes during campaign windows). WHY production and not only sandbox: T2 is ranked threat #1, and sandbox agents don't face production context mixtures; a defense untested against production reality is a hypothesis. Successful injections are scored into `EVALUATOR`'s adversarial suites (`09-technology.md` §17.2) so every red-team win becomes a permanent regression test.
3. **Treasury and gate-integrity exercises:** simulated BEC/social-engineering against gate approvers, attempted envelope-escape chains, gate-shopping attempts — testing the human and constitutional layers, not just software.
4. **External firms annually [ASSUMPTION].** At least one independent external engagement per year: full-scope red team of the platform in odd years, Kernel/Watchdog-focused design review + attack in even years; plus external pen tests per venture per §15. External findings go to the Audit & Risk Committee unfiltered — not summarized by the systems being tested (`SEC-DIR` annotates; the raw report is delivered intact).

### 13.2 Blue team — `BLUE-CELL`
1. **Detection engineering** as code (§8.3), triage and playbook containment (§9.4), behavioral-baseline ownership (§8.2), canary program (§11.5), and hunt operations over the audit stream.
2. **Purple-team cadence:** monthly joint `RED-CELL`/`BLUE-CELL` exercises replaying recent red-team techniques with detections live; every red technique gets a detection verdict — caught / caught-late / missed — and misses open SLA'd work items (§8.4). Quarterly purple reports (technique coverage, mean-time-to-detect trends) go to the CISO and TSC.
3. `BLUE-CELL` and `RED-CELL` MUST remain separate agents with separate task streams and separate human reviewers — a merged red/blue function grades its own homework.

## 14. Internal security (humans)

1. **Least privilege, provable.** Human standing access is role-based, minimal, and reviewed quarterly (access recertification by each system owner; unrecertified access is auto-revoked). Standing production access is **zero**: default human posture toward prod is read-only dashboards.
2. **Break-glass with recording (binding).** Direct production access (shell, DB console, cloud console mutation) exists only via break-glass: named human, stated reason bound to a ticket/incident, time-boxed credential (≤ 4 h), FIDO2 step-up, **full session recording**, real-time notification to `SEC-DIR` + a second human, and mandatory post-hoc review of the recording within 72 h. Break-glass use outside a declared incident or approved change is a Sev-3 by default. WHY recording: break-glass is where insider risk (T3) and attacker-with-stolen-credentials converge; the deterrent and the forensic record must be intrinsic.
3. **Separation of duties (binding).** No human may both **approve** and **execute** the same treasury movement (§6.3's two-human rule draws from disjoint role sets: gate/finance approvers vs. operations executors). Likewise: no human may approve their own break-glass; no author approves their own change (mirrored for agents in `09-technology.md` §16.4); no gate approver may hold operational credentials for the systems their gate governs, above read-only. The directory encodes these disjoint sets; the Kernel PDP enforces them mechanically rather than procedurally.
4. **Joiner/mover/leaver:** access is directory-driven; leaver offboarding revokes all credentials ≤ 4 h from HR trigger; movers lose old-role access at transfer, not "eventually."
5. **Security culture obligations:** phishing-resistant MFA everywhere (§3.4), device management with disk encryption for any device touching C2+, and annual scenario training for gate approvers specifically on T1 social engineering and on §6.3 co-sign discipline (approvers are the highest-value human targets in the system).

## 15. External security (venture-facing product security)

1. **SDLC requirements per cell (binding baseline).** Every venture product built by `BUILDER`/`PROTO` MUST meet the platform SDLC baseline: threat model at design for any feature touching auth, money, or C3 data; SAST/dependency scanning in CI (blocking on critical); secrets scanning; authenticated-scan DAST in staging; security requirements inherited from the cell template (managed auth libraries, §5 field-level encryption for C3, egress allowlists per `09-technology.md` §5.3). The baseline is enforced as `RELEASE` pipeline policy — a venture cannot opt out by moving fast, because the pipeline is the only path to prod.
2. **Pen test before G-06 launch (binding).** Clearing **G-06** (Launch/GTM) REQUIRES a penetration test of the venture's externally-facing surface — internal (`RED-CELL`, distinct engagement from §13.1 platform work) for standard-risk ventures; **external firm** for ventures handling payments directly, regulated data, or C3 at scale **[ASSUMPTION]** (criteria ratified by CISO) — with critical/high findings remediated or formally risk-accepted by the human G-06 approver in the DR. An unresolved critical finding is a G-06 block, full stop.
3. **Bug bounty at scale [ASSUMPTION].** A coordinated vulnerability disclosure policy (security.txt, safe-harbor language, GC-approved) exists for every launched venture from day one; a paid bug bounty program opens per venture when it exceeds materiality thresholds (revenue or user-count, set by CISO + `FIN-DIR` human chain) or handles payments — expected around portfolio year 3. Bounty intake routes into §9 triage; bounty payments are ordinary envelope spend under `SEC-DIR`'s budget (`08-finance.md`).
4. **Venture-customer trust surface:** each launched venture publishes a security page (data handling, subprocessors, disclosure policy) maintained through G-17-approved templates; incidents affecting a venture's customers follow §9.3 without exception — a small venture's breach is handled with the same R4 discipline as the platform's.
5. **Acquired ventures (G-12):** an acquisition's stack enters the portfolio only through a security integration plan in the DD pack — minimum: identity-plane integration, Kernel mediation for any agent operation on its systems, §7 supply-chain review, and a §15.2-equivalent pen test before its systems interconnect beyond a quarantined enclave.

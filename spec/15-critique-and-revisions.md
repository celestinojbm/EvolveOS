# EvolveOS Specification — Part XV: Critique & Revisions

**Status:** Draft v0.1 · **Change class:** R3 (critique record); adopted revisions carry the change class of the part they amend

This part records the external panel review of the EvolveOS specification (Parts 0–XIV and Appendices A–C), the architecture group's disposition of every criticism raised, and the adopted revisions (XV-1 … XV-16) with their binding text. Per `00-overview.md` §9 rule 3, every adopted revision is also recorded as a `> **Revision (XV-n):**` note at the amended location in the affected part.

---

## §1 Method

1. **Independent review.** Eight external panelists, selected for adversarial distance from the project, each reviewed the full specification independently and without access to each other's reviews. Panelists were instructed to attack from their own discipline and to cite the actual text — part, section, and the specific rule or number under criticism — not a paraphrase of it.
2. **Verbatim-style recording.** Critiques are recorded below in the panelist's professional voice, sharp edges intact. Softening a critique in the record would defeat the record's purpose: this part exists so that future readers can judge whether the architecture group answered the strongest version of each objection, not a strawman of it.
3. **Numbered criticisms.** Each critique carries 3–6 numbered criticisms (`CEO-1`, `YC-2`, `ECON-3`, …). The numbering is the unit of disposition: **no numbered criticism may be silently dropped.**
4. **Disposition.** The Founding Architecture Group dispositioned every numbered criticism as exactly one of:
   - **ADOPTED** — the criticism wins; a revision (XV-n) amends the spec.
   - **PARTIALLY ADOPTED** — part of the criticism wins and is folded into a revision; the remainder is answered with reasoning.
   - **DEFERRED** — the criticism is credible but action now would be premature; a **trigger condition** is recorded that forces the question back onto the agenda. A deferral without a trigger is a rejection wearing a costume, and is not permitted.
   - **REJECTED** — the criticism loses, with recorded reasoning. Rejection is a claim the spec must be able to defend; several rejections below are recorded precisely because they may later prove wrong (§5).
5. **Back-propagation.** Every ADOPTED (and adopting-part of PARTIALLY ADOPTED) revision is written into the amended part as an additive `> **Revision (XV-n):**` blockquote. Where the amended location is Constitutional Layer content (`00-overview.md`, `10-security.md`, `11-governance.md`, `appendix-c-decision-gates.md`), the note states the new rule as binding text and the amendment is a G-16 action; this Part XV records the rationale, the Constitutional text carries the law.

## §2 The panel

### §2.1 A Fortune 100 CEO

"I have sat through three decades of operating-model revolutions — matrix organizations, re-engineering, holacracy, 'digital transformation' — and each arrived with a document exactly this confident. So let me be specific rather than dismissive, because parts of this are better than what my own staff produce, and parts of it are a governance mirage.

**CEO-1 — Accountability is nominally assigned and practically diffused.** Part XI §4 makes the CEO the accountable officer for **nineteen** agents — `PRIME`, `PORTFOLIO`, `VENTURE-ORCH`, `EVOLVE`, and fifteen directors — while the CFO owns six and the GC owns five. In my company, an executive 'accountable' for nineteen direct functions is accountable for none of them; the number is the tell. When `V-2027-004` fails publicly, Part XI §11.2 says the gate approver 'owns that judgment,' and the consequence machinery is… an ARC review and possible seat reassignment (§10.3). Who is *fired*? Boards do not accept 'the envelope design was reviewed blamelessly' as an answer when customers were harmed. The one-accountable-human rule (Part III §3.1) is right; the load distribution behind it is not real.

**CEO-2 — The founding governance structure is paper.** Count the humans. Part XIV §13.1: a founding team of 8, with fractional CFO, GC, and CISO in Year 1, and two *advisory* independent committee members. Against that headcount you require: an IC quorum ≥3 (Appendix C, G-07/G-08) chaired by the fractional CFO with the CEO sitting on it; a TSC quorum ≥3 (G-16) that must include the fractional CISO; an ARC 'chaired by an independent director; majority independent' (Part XI §3.3); and a Board of 5–7 with 2–3 independents *plus* an AI-safety-qualified director (Part XI §2.1). At founding, the same four or five people wear every one of these hats, and your only dual-hatting rule is 'no member may chair both IC and ARC' (§3.2). This is precisely the 'oversight theater' your own Part I anti-principle A-4 forbids — quorums that exist in the minutes and not in the world.

**CEO-3 — G-16's two signatures can be one will.** Appendix C requires 'TSC quorum (≥3 humans) + CEO sign-off' for constitutional change, and Part XI §3.4 permits the CEO to *chair the TSC*. You assert the quorum decision and the sign-off are 'separate acts.' In any real boardroom, a chair who also holds a mandatory personal sign-off dominates the session. The separation must be structural — the CEO out of the quorum — or it is decorative.

**CEO-4 — Customer trust is priced too cheaply.** Part XI §6.1(4) requires AI disclosure only 'upon asking,' with proactive disclosure a SHOULD for relational interactions. Your `SUPPORT` agent handles tier-1/2 for every venture at A3 (Appendix B). The day a journalist discovers that grieving or angry customers were counseled by an undisclosed machine — and that day comes — the §6 trust thesis, which you correctly call the asset the whole portfolio shares, takes the write-down. A Fortune-100 brand discloses first and eats the conversion cost, because the alternative is eating the brand.

**CEO-5 — The learning-rate promise smells like a consulting deck.** O-5 demands a portfolio learning rate ≥ +4% per quarter sustained over any rolling 8 quarters (Part I §4) — roughly 17% compounding improvement per year, indefinitely. Every transformation program I have ever bought promised a curve like that; none delivered it past year two. I will grant you this: unlike the consultants, you pre-registered your own kill criteria (K-1 through K-7, Part I §6), and K-1 explicitly falsifies the thesis if the curve is flat for 8 quarters. That is more honest than my staff. It does not make the number less of a wish."

### §2.2 A YC Partner

"You've written 6,300 lines of specification, 68 agent definitions, 19 gates, and a constitutional court — and you have zero users. This is the most beautifully engineered way to avoid finding out whether anyone wants what you make that I have ever reviewed.

**YC-1 — You built the factory before proving you can make one car.** Part XIV Phase 0 spends five quarters (2026-Q4 → 2027-Q4) and $3.2M of the $10M (§13.2) building a Kernel with SPIFFE identities, Merkle-anchored audit logs, and a policy engine, before first revenue arrives at M4 (2027-09, if you're lucky). The one thing venture history is unambiguous about: teams that ship a product learn; teams that ship infrastructure for hypothetical products build very good infrastructure for products nobody wants. Your own Part XIV names 'platform-before-product death spiral' as a Phase 0 dominant risk (§2.1) — naming a disease you are actively contracting is not a mitigation. Run ONE venture, by hand, all the way through, before you industrialize anything.

**YC-2 — The funnel math is terrible economics dressed as discipline.** Part V §4: ~1,000 opportunity briefs → 2 formed ventures per year, at ~$5.5M expected annual pipeline consumption. That is $2.75M of process per seed-stage company. A good founding team gets to the same place — a seeded company with early PMF evidence — on a $500k pre-seed, because they don't pay for 998 corpses and a constitutional apparatus. You call the corpses 'the moat' (Part VI §10). I call most of them ideas a decent partner meeting would have killed in four minutes for free.

**YC-3 — Founder quality is the actual alpha, and EvolveOS has no founder.** Everything we know about outlier outcomes says the variance is in the people: the irrational persistence, the taste, the willingness to do things that look wrong on every rubric. Your pipeline optimizes rubric-legibility by construction — G-03 verdicts against pre-registered criteria, `VENTURE-ORCH` at A3 inside an envelope (Appendix B), PMF measured as LTV:CAC ≥ 3 and M3 retention ≥ 80% (Part V stage 12). Airbnb fails your Stage-2 timing model. Every gate you add selects *against* the illegible outlier that pays for the portfolio. Your own P-6 says outcomes are heavy-tailed; your machinery systematically truncates the tail you need.

**YC-4 — Speed is the startup's only structural weapon, and you've sold it.** G-05/G-06 land in a weekly human slot capped at 3 decisions per session (Part V §6). The ANALYSIS block WIP limit is 8 because of GC review throughput (Part V §5). The decision engine budgets 5 business days for an R3 brief (Part VII §13.1). A live price change queues at `PRICER`'s A1 lane. Meanwhile the two founders in a garage competing with your venture ship the pricing change over lunch. You will lose race after race and your dashboards will say 'gate SLA: 95% attained.'

**YC-5 — Premature generality everywhere.** A 23-stage state machine, WIP limits per macro-state, monthly validation cohorts, conversion rates to two significant figures (Part V §4–§6) — all encoded before a single observation. You admit it yourselves: '[UNCERTAIN] The G-04→G-05 (67%) and G-06→G-07 (33%) rates have the widest error bars.' Everything downstream of those rates — capital plan, WIP limits, staffing — is derived from numbers you invented. The honest version of this spec is one page long: 'do one venture, write down what happened, then design the pipeline.'"

### §2.3 A Nobel Prize economist

"The specification is unusually econometrically literate for a business document — it cites proper scoring rules, real options, and value of information correctly. Its problems are the ones that literacy cannot fix: identification, selection, and a portfolio-level factor exposure the authors have not priced.

**ECON-1 — The counterfactual ledger has an identification problem it then ignores.** Part VII §11.3 states the selection biases honestly — proxies observable only for paths someone else pursued, competitor success not establishing your counterfactual, survivorship in proxy sources. But §11.2 then feeds proxy scores into *calibration* (§7.3) and into kill-criteria re-thresholding. You never observe the road not taken; a competitor's traction is a different treatment applied to a different unit. Using confounded proxies to adjust the weights that steer capital does not 'correct directional bias' — it imports the confounder into the weighting with a confidence label attached. Your own §11.3 caveat ('never as stand-alone justification') is not enforced anywhere in the data flow.

**ECON-2 — Survivorship is baked into the learning loop.** M-2 precision is measured on cohorts that cleared earlier gates; the knowledge store compounds patterns from ventures that survived to generate KIs; RISK-EMRG-03 concedes the intake machinery herds toward categories with early wins. The portfolio learning rate (M-1) will therefore show 'improvement' partly attributable to the sample selecting itself. K-2's pre-registered shadow baseline is a genuinely good control — my compliments — but it tests the *verdict* layer, not the knowledge layer that conditions every verdict.

**ECON-3 — Goodhart on calibration-weighted consensus.** Part VII §9.2 sets wᵢ ∝ 1/(ε + B̄ᵢ). The log score is strictly proper per prediction, yes — but the *portfolio of prediction opportunities* is agent-chosen. An optimizer facing this weighting learns to be present on easy, high-base-rate questions and structurally quiet on hard ones. You mandate coverage for R2+ DR predictions (P-7), which bounds the worst of it, but nothing scores the *selection* of what gets predicted, and your consensus weights are exchangeable across regimes they were never measured in.

**ECON-4 — Principal–agent slippage between agents and the portfolio objective.** Each agent optimizes its card eval metrics (Part IV §4); the portfolio optimizes log-wealth under a survival constraint (Part VIII §14). These objectives are aligned only if the metric-design layer prices cross-agent externalities, and your A-7 counter-metric rule (Part I §9) assigns no owner, no cadence, and no budget to counter-metrics with anything like the rigor the primary metrics get. Unowned counter-metrics decay first — your own RISK-UNK-02 says so.

**ECON-5 — The knowledge moat has an efficient-markets problem.** If validation signals cost ≤ $10k to acquire (G-02 envelope), they are cheap for everyone, and their alpha is arbitraged by whoever else runs the experiment. MOAT-2's claim that 'no amount of competitor capital can buy time-series decision data' is true only of data about *your own* funnels and failures — which is precisely the data least transferable to new categories. The spec marks transferability [UNCERTAIN] in MOAT-2; I would put it more strongly: the moat is real exactly where it is least valuable, and speculative exactly where the thesis needs it.

**ECON-6 — The entire portfolio is one levered bet on a single macro factor.** Every venture's cost structure (agent labor), the platform's own economics (Part XIV §13.3 brackets AI cost as the dominant unknown), the competitive environment (RISK-MKT-01: the advantage is 'rented, not owned'), and the correlated-failure surface (RISK-AI-02) all load on one factor: **the price and capability of frontier AI models**. Part VIII §8.5 sets a 40% correlation limit on ventures sharing a 'dominant risk factor' — yet the factor that dominates *all of them simultaneously* is not a named, limited, stress-tested exposure anywhere in the factor model. You believe you hold twenty bets. You hold one, with twenty expressions."

### §2.4 A distributed systems architect

"The architecture is more honest than most about its centralization — Part XIII opens by itemizing the price. My criticisms are about where the honesty stops short of engineering.

**DSA-1 — 'Small and heavily reviewed' is not a correctness argument for the Kernel.** RISK-SPOF-02 correctly identifies a fail-open Kernel defect as the severe case: it 'silently voids the autonomy–reversibility matrix.' The mitigations are review, red-teaming, and audit reconciliation — all *empirical* checks on a component whose failure mode is defined as the one that empirical checks miss (it fails open *silently*). The invariants at stake — envelope slices form a shrink-only lattice (Part IV §8.3), every over-envelope action converts to A1 rather than executing (Appendix C mechanic 3), one macro-state per venture (Part V §1.1), stop-asymmetry on restart — are small, discrete, and exactly the class of property TLA+/Alloy-style model checking handles routinely. There is no formal model anywhere in Part IX. A pen test (D5's `RED-CELL` pass) samples the behavior space; a model checker exhausts it. For the component you call constitutional, you have chosen sampling.

**DSA-2 — The event log is a global bottleneck with an unmeasured write path.** Part VI §1 makes a single append-only log the source of truth and admits '[UNCERTAIN] The write-amplification cost at portfolio scale is estimated, not measured.' Part IX §22.2 plans ~50k events/s and strongly consistent, per-envelope-serialized spend meters (§9.1) — meaning every venture envelope is a serialization point for all of its parallel workers. Cross-partition causality is carried only 'via task-contract references' (VI §13.1), yet the anti-fragmentation rule (Part VII §2.4) requires scanning same-actor/same-target action sequences over 30-day windows — a cross-partition join running continuously against your busiest stream. Nobody has built this and measured it; the whole learning thesis sits on it.

**DSA-3 — Policy evaluation on every tool call needs a brownout story, not just a fail-closed story.** You budget PDP p99 ≤ 25 ms added latency (IX §15.1) with server-side revocation checks on every call and caching prohibited for money/R3+/stripped-provenance decisions (IX §22.2.1). Hard-down is specified: fail closed, queue, fleet drops to A1 (Part III §4.20). But what about the 250 ms PDP that is not down? Degradation, not outage, is the common failure, and a 10× slower PDP makes 2,000–5,000 agents 10× slower with no specified load-shedding priority.

**DSA-4 — Cell isolation is undermined by the shared platform's correlated blast radius.** Cells are exemplary (IX §2). But all 100 cells share the Kernel, the backbone, the knowledge system, and the ledger through the five Platform Interface surfaces (§2.2). A bad policy-bundle deployment or a poisoned portfolio-scope KI is a simultaneous all-venture event — correlated by construction. Agent changes get shadow mode, canaries, and rollback windows (Part XII §4); platform policy bundles get a CI pipeline.

**DSA-5 — The restart path deadlocks under its own quorum arithmetic.** Appendix C rule 6: restart 'requires the owning gate's full approver set.' Fine for a venture-scoped stop. Now walk the Sev-1 case: a Watchdog trips a *system-wide* G-00 at 03:00. Which gate 'owns' the whole system? If the answer is G-16's TSC quorum + CEO, or a G-07-style IC + GC set, you have specified a multi-day committee process to restart an infrastructure outage whose stop took five seconds. Part X §12.1 nobly requires that governance be operable on paper during a stop — but paper does not change the quorum arithmetic, and every hour of a stopped portfolio is customer harm your own Part X §1 ranks *last*. Stop asymmetry is right; this implementation of it is a self-inflicted denial of service.

**DSA-6 — No machine-checkable statement of the state machine exists anywhere.** Part V draws a Mermaid diagram; Part IX writes illustrative Rego. Between them there is no artifact a tool can verify — no formal spec of gate transitions, envelope algebra, or the A→A1 conversion semantics. For a ten-year system whose central claim is 'the Kernel enforces the Constitution,' the Constitution's operational semantics exist only in prose."

### §2.5 An AI researcher

"The spec takes agent risk more seriously than most production systems I review — provenance labels, privilege stripping, and the measurer/improver separation are genuinely good. The criticisms below are where the threat model is right and the mitigation is a modal verb.

**AIR-1 — Correlated failure across 68 agents on one base model gets a SHOULD.** RISK-AI-02 contains the single best paragraph in the document: 'an N-agent consensus of one model is one opinion with N signatures.' And then the mitigation: `AI-DIR` **SHOULD** route checking functions to a different base model, 'cost permitting.' The highest-ranked structural failure in your own top-10 table (#3) is mitigated by a recommendation with a budget escape hatch. Likewise RISK-SPOF-05's warm second source is tagged [ASSUMPTION]. Part IX §22.3 requires ≥2 qualified providers per tier — qualified on paper. Nothing binds a *tested, golden-suite-passing, actually-failed-over* second source for the agents that matter (T1/T2, and the checkers: `EVALUATOR`, `RISK-QUANT`).

**AIR-2 — Calibration scores assume an exchangeability that venture work violates.** Part VII §7.3 aggregates Brier/log scores per decision class with a 180-day decay; §9.2's cold-start shrinkage triggers only below 20 scored predictions *in the class*. But 'gate_transition' is not a stationary task distribution — every new venture is a domain shift. An agent calibrated on 60 B2B-SaaS predictions receives full weight on its first healthcare-marketplace call, where its calibration is unknown. The consensus machinery will confidently weight assessors on exactly the decisions — novel domains — where the weights are least valid. Distribution shift is the norm of this workload, and your weighting treats it as an exception.

**AIR-3 — The provenance-taint model leaks through paraphrase.** Part X §11.1 is right that 'quotation does not launder provenance.' But taint propagation needs a detectable derivation, and semantic influence has none: an injected P4 document shifts an agent's framing; the agent later proposes a KI *in its own words*, from 'its own analysis,' with clean P2 provenance. Privilege stripping (§11.2) applies while P4 is in context — one summarization hop later, the context is gone and the influence is not. Your defense-in-depth (triangulation, single-source confidence caps) helps; the claim that labels 'close' the summarize-then-trust escalation overstates what the mechanism can see.

**AIR-4 — Evaluation contamination: the golden suites will become training targets.** Part XII §12.3–§12.4 (human-reviewed suite changes, held-out reserves) closes the deliberate channel. The diffuse channel stays open: `AI-DIR` runs fine-tuning programs (Part IV card), `PROMPT-SMITH` mines failure-case archives, and nothing anywhere prohibits benchmark or held-out content from entering a fine-tuning corpus or an optimization context. Over years, suite content leaks into the substrate the way test sets always do, and your regression scores inflate while measuring less. The §15 invariant list — otherwise admirably paranoid — does not contain the sentence 'eval data never enters training data.'

**AIR-5 — The anthropomorphic org chart may be the wrong decomposition entirely.** You mapped agents to a 2005 corporate directory: a CFO's department of `LEDGER`/`TREASURER`/`FPA`, a CMO's of `CONTENT`/`LIFECYCLE`. Models do not have human specialization boundaries; the marginal cost of one agent knowing 'finance and marketing' is zero. The tier hierarchy imports human coordination costs — Part IV §1.2 concedes the extra hops as an [ASSUMPTION] — without importing the human cognitive limits that justified them. The registry-is-exhaustive property ('every actor is one of these 68 definitions,' §5.1) buys auditability at the price of constitutionalizing a job-title taxonomy that is a skeuomorph.

**AIR-6 — Compute is the native currency of agent damage, and your reversibility taxonomy prices only dollars and days.** Part 0 §5 classifies by dollar cost and undo duration; Part IX §22.3 quietly does the right thing operationally ('cognition is metered like money, because it is money' — token budgets as envelope dimensions). But the constitutional classifier itself never says so: an agent can burn unbounded inference on R1-labeled work, and a spawn-runaway's damage accrues in tokens hours before it posts as dollars. The taxonomy that everything else derives from should name compute explicitly."

### §2.6 A cybersecurity expert

"Part X is above the bar for the industry — fail-closed posture, no-model-in-enforcement, secrets never in context. So I will spend my time on the surfaces the document is thinnest about: the humans and the recovery paths, which is where I would attack you.

**SEC-1 — The human approver is your softest target, and R4 approvals don't require out-of-band verification.** Your own register says it: RISK-HUM-03 names deepfake-grade CEO impersonation as 'a present-day capability, not speculation.' The mitigation? Out-of-band confirmation 'for R3+ approvals initiated through unusual channels' — conditional on the attacker being polite enough to use an unusual channel. Treasury movements get hardware co-signature over a transaction digest (X §6.3); that is the right pattern. But G-07 entity formation, G-12 acquisitions, G-13 restructurings, and G-16 constitutional changes clear on step-up FIDO2 inside a session (X §3.4). A phished or coerced approver session, or a fabricated 'urgent' pack that looks channel-normal, meets no second-channel check at exactly the gates where mistakes are irreversible. Extend the treasury discipline to every R4 signature, unconditionally.

**SEC-2 — G-00's one-human stop is also a one-human denial-of-service weapon.** Any authorized human can halt the entire system (Appendix C, G-00); restart requires a full approver set. Correct asymmetry against runaway automation — and a gift to an insider, a coerced officer, or an attacker holding one set of credentials: repeated system-wide halts at near-zero cost to the attacker and full-portfolio cost to you. There is no invocation rate review, no pattern monitoring on stop authority, no distinction between a venture-scope and a system-scope stop's restart path. Availability is your fifth priority (X §1), but 'an adversary can turn the company off weekly with one credential' is not an availability nuance, it's a design gap.

**SEC-3 — The recovery paths are the real attack surface, and they get one bullet point each.** Split knowledge for treasury keys (X §6.3) is sound *in steady state*. The attack goes where the splitting reverses: key-recovery ceremonies reassemble Shamir shards (X §6.4, 3-of-5 among named officers) — who observes that room, what is recorded, what stops a ceremony being socially engineered into existence? And Part X §12.1's continuity-of-control mode has humans executing treasury manually at banking counterparties precisely when `FRAUD-WATCH`, the output filters, and the Kernel's dual-control plumbing are dark. Your degraded mode is my preferred operating window; the spec hardens the front door and leaves the fire exit on a latch.

**SEC-4 — Your change-class boundaries are ambiguous, and ambiguity in change control is an attack surface.** Part XII is 'R3 (except §15 … R4 via G-16).' Part VII is R3 with IC-ratification carve-outs in a header note. Part XIV is R3 except where 'D1, D5, D7' and the never-cut list route to G-16. Nothing in Part 0 §4 — which defines the amendment system — recognizes that a document can carry mixed classes, or says who tracks which section is which. An attacker (or an eager `EVOLVE`) amends the R3 wrapper around an R4 core, or 'reorganizes' a protected section into an unprotected file, and the change-control system has no section-level ledger to catch it.

**SEC-5 — Who builds the builder?** X §7 covers artifacts admirably: signed bundles, SLSA-style provenance, admission control. But the Kernel is compiled by a CI system whose signing identities are 'workload identities of the CI system' (§7.3), administered by the same 3–6 platform engineers who operate `platform-security`. Break-glass (§14.2) governs runtime access; nothing equivalent governs the *build pipeline* as a persistent implant path into the enforcement layer. The insider platform engineer is T3 and T5 combined, and your register treats them as separate rows.

**SEC-6 — Separation of duties is arithmetic, and you don't have the humans for it.** X §14.3 encodes SoD as directory set-disjointness, PDP-enforced. Disjoint sets require enough elements. With 8 people and fractional officers (XIV §13.1), the approver/executor/auditor sets *cannot* be disjoint without the same person appearing in two of them under different hats. Mechanical enforcement of an unsatisfiable constraint means either the constraint is waived quietly or the company deadlocks; both are worse than admitting the founding configuration and bounding it explicitly."

It is not a venture-scale return, and nobody should fund it under the impression that it is."

### §2.8 A regulatory lawyer

"The specification is refreshingly non-delusional about legal personhood (Part XI §7.1 is correct on every point). Its exposure is in the gap between the theory it declares and the operational facts it also, meticulously, documents.

**LAW-1 — The 'sophisticated tools under delegated human authority' theory (XI §7.2) is untested and jurisdiction-fragile.** It is a characterization, not a shield. Courts look through labels to function: `SUPPORT` resolves disputes and grants concessions at A3; `FRAUD-WATCH` freezes consumer transactions automatically; `VENTURE-ORCH` runs a company inside an envelope. Where agents functionally exercise discretion, agency-law and negligent-supervision theories will attach regardless of the 'tool' label — and your DR system, the spec's greatest pride, is a plaintiff's discovery goldmine establishing exactly what the entity knew about its agents' discretion, in immutable, hash-chained form. I do not say abandon the theory; I say it will hold differently in Delaware, Karlsruhe, and Singapore, and the spec treats it as one theory for all jurisdictions.

**LAW-2 — The EU AI Act captures several of your agents as high-risk, and no artifact maps this.** `RECRUITER` performs recruitment sourcing and screening — Annex III employment territory, with conformity, transparency, and human-oversight obligations attaching to deployers. `FRAUD-WATCH`'s automated holds on consumer transactions sit adjacent to creditworthiness/essential-services classifications. `PRICER`'s dynamic pricing and `LIFECYCLE`'s targeting implicate consumer-protection and automated-decision rules. Part XI §7.3 gestures at 'jurisdiction-conditional caps' and `REG-WATCH` tracking, and Part III §4.6 maintains compliance maps *per venture* — but nothing maintains the map that regulators will actually ask for: **per agent**, which regulated activity, which jurisdiction, which obligation, which control.

**LAW-3 — UDAP/FTC exposure on AI-negotiated consumer transactions.** §6.1(2)'s dark-pattern bans are good. But the FTC's live theory includes undisclosed AI interaction itself as deceptive in consumer contexts, and several states already have bot-disclosure statutes. Your §6.1(4) makes disclosure available 'upon asking.' A churn-save offer negotiated by `LIFECYCLE`, a concession granted by `SUPPORT`, a quote assembled by `DEALDESK` — each is an AI-conducted consumer transaction where reactive-only disclosure is below the emerging enforcement norm. You are voluntarily below a rising floor, in writing.

**LAW-4 — Licensure boundaries are managed by supervision that your own spec predicts will erode.** `CONTRACTS` drafts instruments; `SUPPORT` advises customers; `TREASURER` prepares what a regulator may view as investment decision-making. The control is A1 human review under GC (XI §7.2.3) — but Part XIII (RISK-HUM-01, RISK-SPOF-04) predicts, correctly, that a fractional GC reviewing everything becomes the rubber stamp your own instrumentation exists to detect. Unauthorized practice of law is a *criminal* matter in several states; the spec has no register of licensed-profession boundaries per agent per jurisdiction, and no plan for the volume at which 'human counsel reviews everything' stops being true.

**LAW-5 — Cross-border data flows versus the portfolio knowledge pool.** Part VI §8 gates raw personal data well. The load-bearing move is the promotion of 'aggregated, de-identified insights' to PC-1 for portfolio-wide reuse. GDPR's anonymization standard is far stricter than de-identification; a willingness-to-pay curve derived from 20 EU interviews may remain personal data, and its portfolio-wide reuse a cross-border transfer plus purpose change. The transfer analysis (SCCs, adequacy, TIAs) appears nowhere, and `PRIVACY`'s review of 'the aggregation before promotion' (VI §8) cites no standard it reviews against.

**LAW-6 — The Board's reliance on agent-generated DRs undermines its own defense.** Directors deciding G-12 or G-14 will rely on `MNA-ANALYST` DD packs and `FIN-MODEL` valuations. Reasonable-reliance doctrine (e.g., DGCL §141(e)) protects reliance on *officers and experts* — persons. Part XI §2.3 hands directors 'unrestricted read access to all DRs,' and Part III §3.2 says agents never brief the Board except through officers — good instinct, incomplete formality. What is missing is the ritual that makes the defense work: a named accountable officer *adopting* each board-decided gate pack as their own professional product, on the record, before the vote. Without it, you have built a system in which the directors' best defense is weaker than at a conventional company — while taking irreversible decisions faster."

## §4 Adopted revisions

Each revision states: what changes, where, the new binding text or rule, and why the critique won. Revisions amending Constitutional Layer documents (Parts 0/X/XI, Appendix C) are R4 changes adopted via the G-16 path; others carry the change class of the amended part.

### XV-1 · Emergency restart quorum for infrastructure-scope G-00 stops; stop-authority abuse review
**Source:** DSA-5, SEC-2. **Amends:** `appendix-c-decision-gates.md` (gate mechanics; Constitutional, G-16).
**New binding rule.** Gate mechanic 6 (stop asymmetry) is amended as follows:
1. G-00 stops whose scope is a **venture, gate-owned process, or single decision** restart only via the owning gate's full approver set (unchanged).
2. G-00 stops whose scope is **infrastructure-level** — an agent class, a platform component, or the whole system — MAY be restarted by an **emergency restart quorum** of three humans: the CISO, the accountable officer of the stopped scope (or the CEO for system scope), and one Executive Committee member who neither triggered the stop nor is the accountable officer — after review of the triggering evidence bundle. Every emergency restart MUST be ratified by the scope's owning approver set within **5 business days**; absent ratification, the stop re-engages automatically (Kernel-enforced).
3. **Stop-authority pattern review:** two or more system-scope G-00 invocations by the same principal within 30 days without ARC-validated cause trigger a mandatory ARC review of that principal's invocations. Review only — stop authority is never suspended by default, because hesitation to stop is the costlier failure.
**Why the critique won.** Appendix C rule 6 as written specified a multi-day committee process to restart a five-second stop, and gave any single credential holder a repeatable portfolio-wide denial-of-service. The asymmetry survives — stopping still takes one human and restarting still takes a quorum plus ratification — but the quorum is now reachable at 03:00 and the abuse channel is watched.

### XV-2 · Out-of-band verification and hardware co-signature for all R4 approvals; recovery-path dual control
**Source:** SEC-1, SEC-3 (and RISK-HUM-03). **Amends:** `10-security.md` §3.4, §6.4, §12.1 (Constitutional, G-16); cross-referenced in `appendix-c-decision-gates.md` gate mechanics.
**New binding rule.**
1. Every human approval of an **R4 gate decision** (all R4 rows of Appendix C, including G-16) MUST be executed as: (a) a **hardware-security-key signature over the decision digest** (extending the X §6.3 treasury co-sign mechanism to all R4 gates), and (b) **out-of-band confirmation on a pre-registered second channel** whenever the approval request, its urgency claim, or any material input reached the approver through any channel other than the Kernel-rendered gate queue. A session-authenticated click is never sufficient for R4.
2. **Key-recovery ceremonies** (X §6.4) and **degraded-mode manual treasury operations** (X §12.1) MUST operate under two-person integrity with session recording and mandatory post-hoc ARC review — the degraded mode inherits the dual-control rules, not merely the access paths. A recovery ceremony convened outside the documented calendar or without a declared triggering incident is itself a Sev-2.
**Why the critique won.** The register already named the attack (deepfaked authority, RISK-HUM-03) and the strongest defense already existed for treasury movements; leaving entity formation, M&A, restructuring, and constitutional change on weaker verification than a wire transfer was an inversion of the spec's own severity ordering. SEC-3's point stands on its own: the moments when split knowledge reassembles and when the automated controls are dark are precisely when human-anchored controls must be strongest.

### XV-3 · Mixed-change-class documents recognized in Part 0 §4
**Source:** SEC-4. **Amends:** `00-overview.md` §4 (Constitutional, G-16).
**New binding rule.** A specification document MAY carry mixed change classes only by **explicit section-level designation declared in the document's header**. Designated protected sections are Constitutional Layer content: they amend only via G-16, and change control (the Kernel's document-integrity checks and the G-16 history) tracks class at section level, not file level. Undesignated content inherits the document's base class; ambiguity resolves to the higher class. Moving content between documents never changes its class. The v0.1 mixed-class designations are ratified as: Part VII (header carve-outs a–b), Part VIII (header carve-outs a–b), Part XII §15, Part XIV §11 (D1/D5/D7) and §13.4 never-cut list.
**Why the critique won.** The spec had already, correctly, embedded R4-protected islands inside R3 documents — but the amendment system of Part 0 §4 did not know such islands existed, leaving a wrapper-editing path around G-16. Change-class ambiguity in the layer that defines change control is a self-referential hole; it is now closed at the definitional level.

### XV-4 · G-16 approver independence: the CEO is out of the TSC quorum
**Source:** CEO-3. **Amends:** `appendix-c-decision-gates.md` G-16 row and `11-governance.md` §3.4 (Constitutional, G-16).
**New binding rule.** For gate G-16, the TSC quorum (≥3 humans) MUST be counted **excluding the CEO**. The CEO's sign-off remains required and remains a separate act; the CEO MAY attend the G-16 session but MUST NOT chair it or count toward its quorum. Part XI §3.4 is amended accordingly: a G-16 session is chaired by an independent director whenever one sits on the TSC, and never by the CEO.
**Why the critique won.** Two constitutionally separate acts performed under one person's chairmanship are one act with two timestamps. The whole point of pairing a quorum with a sign-off is two independent failure modes; dual-hatting collapsed them.

### XV-5 · Compute/token budgets as a first-class envelope dimension in the constitutional taxonomy
**Source:** AIR-6. **Amends:** `00-overview.md` §5 (Constitutional, G-16); glossary *Envelope* entry (`appendix-a-glossary.md`).
**New binding rule.** The worst-case-cost basis of the R1–R4 classifier explicitly includes **committed compute/inference cost at metered prices**, and every envelope carries compute/token budgets as a first-class dimension alongside spend, tools, data classes, action types, and rate limits (as Part IX §22.3 already implements). Exhausting a compute budget converts the agent's actions to A1 exactly as a money-envelope breach does. Compute is money that has not posted yet; the constitutional taxonomy now says so rather than leaving it to an implementation section.
**Why the critique won.** Part IX had already metered cognition like money; Part 0 — the document everything else derives from — classified only dollars and days. An agent's damage in a runaway accrues in tokens hours before it books as dollars; the load-bearing taxonomy should not lag its own enforcement layer.

### XV-6 · Base-model vendor concentration: binding warm second source and drilled failover for T1/T2 and checker agents
**Source:** AIR-1 (and ECON-6, RISK-SPOF-05, RISK-AI-02). **Amends:** `09-technology.md` §22.3; `13-failure-analysis.md` RISK-SPOF-05 and RISK-AI-02 (R3).
**New binding rule.**
1. For every agent class serving **T1 or T2 agents**, and for the checker agents **`EVALUATOR`, `RISK-QUANT`, `RED-CELL`**, a second qualified model vendor MUST hold a **current golden-suite pass** (re-validated at every suite version change) — warm, not merely contracted.
2. Provider-failover drills MUST execute an **actual routing cutover** for a sampled subset of these classes at least quarterly, with results reported to the TSC. A failed drill is a Sev-3 with a fix SLA.
3. The RISK-AI-02 heterogeneity mitigation is upgraded from SHOULD to **MUST**: `EVALUATOR` judging and `RISK-QUANT` verification run on a different base model than the dominant doing-model, unless the TSC grants a documented, annually-renewed waiver.
**Why the critique won.** The register ranked correlated model failure #3 among portfolio-killers and vendor disruption "High within 3 years," then mitigated both with an [ASSUMPTION] and a "SHOULD, cost permitting." Insurance that is optional under cost pressure is not insurance; the modal verbs now match the ranking.

### XV-7 · Phase-0 governance realism: bought independence, dual-hat limits, dated transition
**Source:** CEO-2 (also CEO-1, SEC-6, VC-context). **Amends:** `11-governance.md` §3 (Constitutional, G-16); `14-implementation-roadmap.md` §13.1 (R3).
**New binding rule.**
1. From founding until the first G-07, the IC and TSC quorums MUST **each include at least one compensated external independent member** (neither officer nor employee); the Part XIV §13.1 Y1 independent seats are voting seats, not advisory.
2. **Dual-hat limit:** no individual may sit on more than two of {IC, TSC, ARC}, and no gate approver may review a domain they operate (extending Part III §5.3's reviewer rule to committee duty).
3. **Dated transition:** the first G-07 MUST NOT clear until the Board is seated per XI §2.1 with at least two independent directors and the ARC has an independent chair. Founding-configuration governance is thereby explicitly bounded in time and blocked from taking the first R4 venture decision.
**Why the critique won.** The CEO's arithmetic was simply correct: 8 people with fractional officers cannot staff the written quorums independently, and pretending otherwise at founding would inaugurate the system with exactly the oversight theater A-4 prohibits. The revision buys real independence early (two external voting members is cheap), bounds the unavoidable dual-hatting, and makes full independence a hard precondition of the first irreversible venture act rather than an aspiration.

### XV-8 · Capital structure and returns model: mandatory, IC-ratified, before any G-08 tranche
**Source:** VC-2, VC-3, VC-4 (VC-1 partially). **Amends:** `08-finance.md` §14 (R3, with the header's IC-ratification carve-out applying); `14-implementation-roadmap.md` §10 (R3).
**New binding rule.** Before the first G-08 tranche clears, the CFO and `FIN-DIR` MUST present, and the IC MUST ratify as an R4 DR:
1. The **capital-structure decision**: evergreen HoldCo vs. fund vs. hybrid (with GC), including ownership instruments, the distribution/recycle waterfall, and governance terms — confirming that no instrument grants any capital provider a bypass of G-16;
2. The **platform-cost recovery mechanism** (arm's-length platform charges to ventures per XI §9.1, management charge, or equivalent), demonstrating on the Part VIII §6.2 forecast that portfolio economics repay the platform, not only the ventures;
3. A **portfolio returns model** benchmarked against BOTH the vintage-matched VC median (M-4) and **published venture-studio base rates**, with the gap to each stated explicitly.
This ratification is recorded as a milestone (M6b) in Part XIV §10; G-08 is closed until it exists.
**Why the critique won.** The spec had specified the machine to the daily level and left blank what the machine's owners hold and how the platform gets paid. Scale capital deployed before those answers exist would be deployed into an unpriced structure; and the studio-base-rate benchmark is adopted because a document this insistent on base rates must face the base rate of its own organizational form.

### XV-9 · Formal verification of Kernel gate/envelope invariants before treasury automation
**Source:** DSA-1, DSA-6. **Amends:** `09-technology.md` §9.1 (R3, with Kernel-enforcement changes remaining G-16); `14-implementation-roadmap.md` §11 dependency D5 (G-16 per XIV §14).
**New binding rule.** The Kernel's core enforcement invariants — envelope slices form a strictly shrink-only lattice; over-envelope and over-matrix actions convert to A1 (never execute, never fail silently); every venture occupies exactly one macro-state; G-00 stop/restart follows the (amended) stop asymmetry; separation-of-duties set-disjointness — MUST be specified in a **machine-checkable formal model** (TLA+/Alloy-class), with model-checking runs in CI on every change to enforcement semantics. Dependency **D5 is amended**: treasury automation (`TREASURER` beyond A0 on live rails; `FRAUD-WATCH` auto-holds on real money) additionally requires a passing formal model of these invariants, not only the `RED-CELL` pass. A G-16 enforcement-rule change without an updated, passing model is invalid.
**Why the critique won.** The fail-open Kernel defect is defined by RISK-SPOF-02 as the silent, matrix-voiding case — the case empirical testing is worst at finding. The invariants are small and discrete; exhaustive checking is cheap relative to what it protects. "Heavily reviewed" was the weakest sentence in the enforcement story and it guarded the strongest claim.

### XV-10 · Counterfactual ledger epistemics: proxy-scored outcomes are a separate, lower-confidence evidence tier
**Source:** ECON-1 (ECON-2 partially). **Amends:** `07-decision-engine.md` §11 (R3); noted in `06-knowledge-system.md` §10.
**New binding rule.** Proxy-scored counterfactual outcomes constitute a distinct evidence tier, **E-CF (counterfactual, proxy-graded)**:
1. Every E-CF datum carries its proxy-quality grade and the §11.3 bias caveats structurally, not as prose;
2. E-CF scores MUST NOT enter calibration ledgers at parity with realized-outcome scores — their weight in any calibration aggregate is capped at 0.25 relative to realized outcomes **[ASSUMPTION** — initial cap; TSC-tunable on evidence**]**;
3. E-CF evidence MUST NOT be cited as material evidence in R3+ DRs, except in threshold-tuning EPs (kill-criteria re-thresholding per §11.2), where its use and grade MUST be stated in the EP.
**Why the critique won.** §11.3 diagnosed the identification problem and §11.2 then routed the confounded data into the weights that steer capital anyway. The ledger's honest yield — directional bias correction — is preserved; its dishonest use — confounded proxies masquerading as outcomes inside calibration — is closed.

### XV-11 · Regulated-activity and licensure register; officer adoption of board-decided gate packs
**Source:** LAW-2, LAW-4, LAW-6 (LAW-1 partially). **Amends:** `11-governance.md` §8 and §2.3 (Constitutional, G-16).
**New binding rule.**
1. `COMPL-DIR` SHALL maintain a standing **agent-to-regulated-activity register**: for every registered agent, per operating jurisdiction — the regulated activities it touches (including EU AI Act risk classification, e.g., Annex III employment exposure for `RECRUITER`; credit/essential-services adjacency for `FRAUD-WATCH` holds; automated-pricing and consumer-protection exposure for `PRICER`/`LIFECYCLE`), the **licensed-profession boundaries** it operates near (`CONTRACTS` — legal practice; `SUPPORT` — regulated advice; `TREASURER` — investment activity), the obligations triggered, and the ceiling or jurisdiction-conditional caps applied (XI §7.3). The register is reviewed at **every G-11** and at least annually by the GC; a venture's G-06 compliance clearance MUST cite the register entries for every agent instance serving that venture.
2. Every R4 gate pack decided by the **Board or IC** MUST be formally **adopted and attested by its accountable officer** (XI §4) before the vote — the officer presents the material as their own professional product, so that director reliance runs on a human officer/expert as reliance doctrine requires. Agent authorship remains fully documented in the DR; the attestation adds the human adoption, it never obscures the provenance.
**Why the critique won.** The compliance machinery was venture-indexed while the regulatory exposure is agent-indexed; the register closes that axis. The officer-adoption rule costs one signature per R4 pack and repairs the directors' reasonable-reliance posture — the cheapest legal-defense purchase available in the whole document.

### XV-12 · The pathfinder rule: one venture through the whole pipeline, by hand, before parallelism
**Source:** YC-1, YC-5 (partial adoptions). **Amends:** `14-implementation-roadmap.md` §2.1 (R3).
**New binding rule.** Phase 0 designates exactly **one pathfinder venture**. The pathfinder MUST clear each of G-04, G-05, and G-06 — end-to-end, in manual-heavy mode (humans execute, agents draft) — **before any second venture clears the same gate**. Parallel exploration through G-01–G-03 continues unrestricted (it is R1/R2 and cheap by design). Each pathfinder gate passage MUST produce a stage post-mortem KI set *before* the next venture attempts that gate, so the second venture inherits the first's tuition. Horizon-4/5 tables are re-derived under this rule; a ≤ one-quarter slip of M4 attributable to the pathfinder rule is accepted and pre-authorized.
**Why the critique won (and where it lost).** The panelist's demand to skip the constitutional apparatus was rejected — D1/D2 (no autonomous external action before ratified rules and a live Kernel) are the point of the system, not overhead on it. But the observation that every pipeline parameter was invented rather than observed is true, and the spec's own remedy (quarterly re-fitting, V §4) needs observations to fit. The pathfinder rule buys the observations in the cheapest order: serially at the expensive gates, in parallel at the cheap ones.

### XV-13 · Evaluation-contamination invariant: eval data never enters training data
**Source:** AIR-4. **Amends:** `12-self-evolution.md` §15 (R4 via G-16, per that section's own class).
**New binding rule.** Part XII §15 gains invariant 8: **Benchmark, golden-suite, and held-out-reserve content MUST NOT enter any training, fine-tuning, or prompt-optimization corpus.** The exclusion is a Kernel-enforced data-access boundary, not a convention. `AI-DIR` fine-tuning programs MUST publish data manifests; `EVALUATOR` audits manifests against suite content each cycle. A contamination finding voids the affected suite versions, re-baselines the affected agents, and blocks every EP that relied on the contaminated scores until re-benchmarked.
**Why the critique won.** §12.4's held-out rotation defends against agents *seeing* the test; nothing defended against the test diffusing into the substrate through the system's own fine-tuning and prompt-mining loops. The missing sentence is the oldest rule in machine learning, and its absence from an otherwise exhaustive invariant list was an oversight, not a decision.

### XV-14 · Regime-conditional calibration: weights shrink out-of-distribution, and humans see the flag
**Source:** AIR-2, ECON-3 (partial). **Amends:** `07-decision-engine.md` §7.3/§9.2 (R3).
**New binding rule.** Calibration weights are conditioned on decision class **and domain regime** (market class, business-model archetype, jurisdiction band — regime vocabulary owned by `EVALUATOR` with `DATA-DIR`). When a decision lies outside the distribution over which an assessor's calibration was measured — a new venture domain, a new archetype, or a declared regime change — that assessor's weight shrinks toward uniform per the §9.2 cold-start rule *as applied within the regime*, regardless of its global score. Every DR whose assessor panel is majority out-of-regime MUST carry an **out-of-distribution flag**, rendered in the §8.2 human brief. `EVALUATOR` additionally reports, per agent, the coverage of its calibration record across regimes, so that silence on hard regimes is visible.
**Why the critique won.** The weighting machinery was most confident exactly where its evidence was weakest — novel domains, which are the portfolio's steady-state diet. Shrinkage-to-uniform is already the spec's own cold-start answer; this revision applies it where the cold start actually recurs, and tells the human approver when the consensus in front of them is running on borrowed calibration.

### XV-16 · AI disclosure: proactive is mandatory for relational and ongoing consumer interactions

## §5 Rejected and deferred critiques worth recording

The dispositions above are complete; this section preserves the reasoning on the objections most likely to be re-litigated, so future readers argue with the strongest version on record.

1. **The YC "cathedral" critique (YC-1/YC-2/YC-5) — partially adopted, core rejected, and the rejection is a bet.** The panelist's strongest form is: *you cannot know the pipeline's parameters before running it, so don't build the pipeline first.* The architecture group's answer is that EvolveOS's differentiating constraint is not product-market fit but *control*: agents spending money without a ratified constitution and a live Kernel is not a lean experiment, it is the exact failure class Part X exists to prevent (D1/D2 are load-bearing, not ceremonial). What we conceded — fully — is the epistemic point: every V §4 conversion rate is invented, and the spec was structured to start learning in parallel at scale before learning in series at all. The pathfinder rule (XV-12) is the synthesis: constitution first, then *one* car through the factory, by hand, before the assembly line runs. If the pathfinder's gate post-mortems invalidate the pipeline's shape (not just its parameters), Part V is re-drafted — that is an explicit possibility this record acknowledges. YC-3 (founder quality as the alpha) is deferred with a real trigger, not dismissed: if M-2 precision or MVP→G-07 conversion undershoots its floor, the human founder-in-residence variant goes to the EC as an L4 EP, and the spec's implicit claim — that gate discipline plus compounding knowledge can substitute for founder irrationality — will have been falsified in the only court that matters.
2. **ECON-5 (efficient markets vs. the knowledge moat) — rejected, recorded as live.** The rejection rests entirely on the thesis's own kill criteria: MOAT-2 already marks cross-regime transferability [UNCERTAIN], and K-1/K-4 falsify the moat claim if cheap-signal arbitrage equalizes returns. If those criteria trip, this critique is the reason why, and this paragraph is the record that we were told.
3. **AIR-5 (the anthropomorphic org chart) — rejected, recorded as unexplored.** The honest ground for rejection is narrow: the decomposition is accountability-driven (blame assignment, envelope lattices, single-issuer rules — IV §1.2), not a claim about optimal cognitive architecture, and XII §8 permits L4 re-decomposition on evidence. The honest concession is that no non-anthropomorphic decomposition was seriously designed and compared. If coordination overhead (the IV §1.2 [ASSUMPTION]) or persistent cross-domain contract friction shows up in org-review telemetry, the alternative deserves a real design study, not a table row.
4. **DSA-2 (event log as global bottleneck) — deferred on a measurement, not an argument.** The critique may simply be right; nobody knows, which is the point of the trigger. The Phase-1 benchmark (VI §1) now carries a quantified pass bar (≥ 2× the IX §22.2 design point at M1) and a forced L5 EP on failure. Deferral was chosen over redesign because the alternative architectures all sacrifice the replay/audit properties that Parts VI, VII, and XI are built on — a trade that should only be made under measured, not hypothesized, pressure.
5. **SEC-5 (who builds the builder) — deferred into the external audit cycle.** Build-chain compromise of the Kernel is the deepest attack in the register's space and no internal control fully answers it (the insiders under suspicion administer the controls). The existing mitigations (no self-review, SLSA-L3 provenance, break-glass recording) are real but partial; the even-year external Kernel engagement (X §13.1.4) now carries mandatory build-chain scope, and its findings route to G-16. This is the one deferral made not because the risk is remote but because the honest remediation (independent build attestation, reproducible builds verified by a party outside the company) needs external hands.
6. **VC-5 (LP terms vs. the Constitution) — deferred to the only moment it can be tested.** The constraint is already binding (XIV §2.5: external capital MUST NOT acquire the ability to bypass G-16); whether capital exists on those terms is a market question answerable only at M11. The record should be candid about the downside the panelist named: if the answer is no, EvolveOS is a self-funded institution, not a venture-scale return — and Part VIII §14's capital-return machinery, not a narrative pivot, is the required response.
7. **LAW-5 (anonymization and cross-border pooling) — deferred with the tightest trigger in this part.** The first PC-2→PC-1 aggregation promotion or the first GDPR-class G-11 — whichever comes first — forces a named anonymization standard and a transfer analysis as a G-18 precondition. Until then, the pooling machinery of VI §8 operates on the strictest reading: when in doubt, the aggregate is treated as personal data. The panelist's underlying warning — that the knowledge moat and data-protection law pull in opposite directions — is recorded as a standing tension, not a solved problem.
